Home Life Work




Who I Am


Homeworker.

I seldom go out for earning money or working. Relaxation and comfort mean a lot to me when it comes to work. As a homeworker, I have many options and have several jobs to do at the same period of time. Most of them are related to art, music and food.


You can call me a writer, a music composer, a gourmet, a traveller, and even more. I haven't figured out an exact way to define myself yet. I guess "a homeworker" works just well for now. Or you may now think me as a guitar teacher or a director of a music studio, Guitaroof.


I bet lots of people wonder or maybe even hope for a career without having to suffer the stress from outside. It may be a good way to stay inside to keep away from the things that bother. But anyway, I would admit that peace inside is indeed the most important above all.


I was born in Guangdong, China.

I was born in Guangdong, China. I grew up and got my highschool diploma there. I have always loved my life and study experience in my hometown. My family still live there now. I think I will move back to meet with them eventually. I am independent but usually homesick.

This modern city is full of inspiring cultural perspectives. There are plentiful opportunities, such as art-related internship programs and international exchange institutions. Regardless of the excellent educational conditions here in this region, I decided to make some changes in my study life.

With the encouragement of my family, I got the chance of going abroad to explore things related to art and music, which interest me the most. I got my bachelor degree in areas that I have always been into and my surprising master degree in food science, which was one of the later developed interests of mine.


Pratt, NY.

After high school graduation, I came to America for bachelor and master study in the field of art and music. Five years ago, I graduated from Pratt, the Art Institute in Brooklyn, New York. Other than that, I also have earned a master degree in food science.


Adventures.

I had been exploring around for the last few years, changing my jobs for several times in the past few years. I enjoyed this kind of life style, always trying things new. But I also know that it is important to find something to stick with. Last year I finally settled down as, which I preferred to define myself as, a homeworker.

I enjoy staying at home and keep myself away from offices or any outside workplaces. I prefer a quiet and comfortable workspace, especially where I can wear my pajamas. It is not that I do not like to go outside. Instead, I love hanging out for travel, for food, for meeting with friends, and for all other kinds of fun things.

It seems that adventures can take place in all kinds of situations in real life. No matter what kinds of occupations you have in hand or where you are travelling or staying, adventures to me are all about experiencing the dynamics of personal feelings, which is not possibly to be shared with one another.








What I Did


Art and food.

In the past few years, I have worked in art museums as an interior design advisor and a tour guide. Later, I got hired by local food magazines and music magazines as well, writing articles, songs or comments for them, which are the kinds of things that I have continued to do until now.


The TV program.

I used to work in a food-related TV program in New York as one of the producers. It was the year before last year and was a wonderful experience for sure. I got to taste various delicious foods and learn a lot from expertise in the field of food science. But it was in California and I preferred to get back New York at that time.


A change.

I then decided to change my vocation after working for the TV program for one year. Beyond location constraints, I wanted some more challenges, perhaps something new. That was when I started new adventures then.








What I Do


Guitaroof.

I own a music studio, called Guitaroof. You can find out more information from our YouTube channel. This studio is well-known among new music composers nowadays. My colleagues and I are coming up with new ideas this coming Spring for advancement.


Growing.

This studio is well-known among new music composers nowadays. It is landing on several other states as well, including Wisconsin, California, Ohio, North Carolina, Michigan and Florida. I believe that it will continue to grow in the following years, more rapidly.


Gathering.

As the director of the studio, I am always invited to write articles for or make comments on local music magazines and newspapers. It is a sort of job but has been an enjoyment in life for me, too. Through this, I hope to gather people who are passionate about music together.
